About Us

About Us

First of all, Bank of Tomorrow is NOT a real bank. Rather, this website was set up to make people aware of the algorithms underlying banking decisions, like whether a bank will give you a mortgage or not.

AI has been affecting many industries, including banking. Here, we have created a fairly simple Machine Learning (ML) algorithm which will decide whether an individual is entitled to a mortgage. The idea of this project is to let people interact freely with the algorithm, to better understand how such algorithms work, and essentially why banks may be rejecting their mortgage applications. For more information on our algorithm, please read the next section

Our Algorithm

Our Algorithm

The fundamental goal of our algorithm is to detect individuals who are at risk of defaulting on their mortgage. Mortgage defaulting is bad news for any bank, as it involves an often lengthy and expensive legal process, where overall money is lost.

When a bank agrees to give you a mortgage, they are in effect taking some sort of risk. To minimize costs, banks will only want to give mortgages to people who are less likely to default on their mortgage, and so are less 'risky'. In this algorithm, we have worked with a 2017-2018 dataset provided by Freddie Mac in the US. It is one of the few publicly available datasets about mortgage default. We have used this dataset to create an ML algorith, which can detect whether an individual is likely to default on their mortgage or not.

You may (reasonably) ask the question: "How relevant is an algorithm that was created from a 2017-2018 US study, if I want to try to get a mortgage in the UK in 2025?". Every ML model has its limitations, and ours is no exception. In fact, our algorithm was not designed to give you a whole lot of actionable insight. Our idea was to empower consumers, by letting them interact with an ML algorithm that is similar to those used by real banks. This way, consumers can gain insights into how ML banking models work under the hood, and understand the hidden biases in these algorithms.
